Category Guide

Categorize by what the money bought, not why
CategoryCRE examplesTax note
Advertising & MarketingConstant Contact; CoStar / LoopNet / Crexi listing-promotion fees; flyer & brochure printing; drone / aerial listing photography; signage; paid social or Google ads; branded materials.Fully deductible — Schedule C Line 8.
Client Meals & EntertainmentBuyer / seller lunch or dinner; coffee with a prospect; drinks after a property tour; prospecting meals.50% deductible — applied automatically (Line 24b).
Client GiftsClosing gifts; client appreciation baskets; thank-you gifts to referral sources.Capped at $25 per recipient/year (Line 27a). Track recipients separately.
Software & SubscriptionsCoStar; Crexi Pro; LoopNet; underwriting / Excel tools; CRM; Adobe; AI tools; Zoom; e-signature; Reonomy; skip-trace tools.Fully deductible — Line 27a.
Licensing & DuesPA real estate license renewal; MLS dues; board / association memberships; NAR or local Realtor association fees.Fully deductible — Line 23.
Travel & MileageDriving to tours, showings & closings; parking; tolls; flights & hotels for a conference; getting-there costs for networking events.Fully deductible — Line 24a. Track miles separately for IRS standard rate.
Office & SuppliesPrinter; paper; business cards; desk equipment; postage; home-office consumables.Fully deductible — Line 22.
Professional ServicesAccountant; attorney review; bookkeeper; S-corp / entity filing help; 1099 contractors.Fully deductible — Line 17.
Continuing EducationRequired CE credits for license renewal; CRE designation courses (CCIM etc.); industry training.Fully deductible — Line 27a.
OtherBank fees on the business account; anything that genuinely doesn't fit another category.Varies — review with your accountant (Line 27a).

💡 Where does "business development" go?

  • Prospecting lunch with a potential client → Client Meals & Entertainment
  • Direct-mail or email campaign to building owners → Advertising & Marketing
  • Lead-gen or data subscription (Reonomy, skip-trace) → Software & Subscriptions
  • Sponsoring an event with your name / brand on it → Advertising & Marketing
  • Getting to a networking event or conference → Travel & Mileage
  • An accredited course at a conference → Continuing Education
  • Closing gifts / client appreciation → Client Gifts ($25/recipient cap)
The IRS has no "business development" line — it wants to know what the money bought, not why.
